INGREDIENTS

STORAGE AND PREPARATION
Reheating tortillas:
- For best results griddle to order on an un-greased flat top or pan, toast both sides until warmed through and little brown spots appear if desired.
- Use a little spray of water to help re hydrate the tortillas as they reheat if a few days old.
- If necessary tortillas can be kept warm for about 15-20 minutes after reheating by stacking them in a folded clean tea towel, top and serve promptly.

Storage:
Keep refrigerated. Once open, wrap and consume by use by date.

Suitable for freezing (freeze on day of purchase and consume within 1 month).

To cook from frozen, prise one tortilla off at a time and heat in a hot pan on both sides or defrost in the packet overnight in the fridge.

SERVING SUGGESTION
Slow cooked pork tacos with pink pickled onions

Peel and finely slice a small red onion then add a pinch of salt and cover with cider vinegar and a splash of water. Cover and leave to sit until the onions turn pink. Marinate a piece of boneless pork shoulder. Add a little oil to a heavy bottomed casserole dish and brown the meat on all sides, then pour the marinade into the pan, cover and roast at 180°C, for a couple of hours or until completely tender. When cool enough to handle, shred the meat and serve on warmed tacos with the pink pickled onions.
